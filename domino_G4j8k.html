Domino / PlayMates / Grande Domino (Gottlieb, 1968)

Domino is the replay version. PlayMates and Grand Domino are the add-a-ball versions. This guide focuses primarily on Domino, with differences seen in PlayMates mentioned at the end of the guide.
PlayMates is not to be confused with any of the tables themed around the Playboy media franchise, which has pinball adaptations by Bally (1978), Data East (1989), and Stern Pinball (2002).


Quick strategy synopsis

The safest points on this table come from shooting back up toward the top corners trying to get the ball to land in the 500 point saucers. Lit top lanes, the top saucers, lit side targets, and the out lanes change which standup targets and lanes are lit for 100 points, and advance the lit domino. The saucers and the out hole spin the roto-target in the playfield center; hitting a target with a star or with the same number as the lit domino scores 500 points, when all other roto-target values score 100.


Shots and table features

Top lanes

There are 6 top lanes. 2 of them will be lit at any given time. Unlit lanes score 50 points. Lit lanes score 100 points and advance the domino.

Bumpers

The yellow bumpers or the red bumpers will always be lit, but never both sets at once. All bumpers score 1 point, or 10 points when lit.

Upper saucers

Score 500 points, advance the domino, and spin the roto-target in the center of the playfield. These are saucers are also lit for special by advancing the domino the end of the train (14 advances).

Side standup targets

One target in each group of three will be lit. Unlit standup targets score 50 points. Lit standup targets score 100 points and advance the domino.

Center roto-target

The center fan-shaped target scores 50 points, or 100 when lit. The panels on either side of the fan target (which are the ones that actually spin) score 100 points, or 500 points if the panel is a star or if the panel's number matches the currently lit domino.

Bottom of the table

Domino has no in lanes. The flippers back up directly to the slingshots. Out lanes score 100 points, and are lit for a special by advancing the domino to the end of the track. When the ball drains and lands in the out hole, the roto-target will spin to a new position.

When are things lit?

The left three top lanes, right three top lanes, left standup targets, and right standup targets each form sets of three features. One lane or target within each of these groups of three will be lit for 100 points and an advance. Earning a 100-point award + a domino advance from the lit lane/target, or entering the saucer, or going down and out lane, will rotate which lane or target in each of those groups of three is lit, and will also switch whether the red or yellow bumpers are lit.

The center fan target is lit on rotation with the groups of three mentioned above, meaning it will be lit 1/3 of the time.

Lit white lanes/targets and top saucers advance the domino. The lit domino value proceeds in this order: 5-3-4-1-2-3-4-2-1-5-3-1-4-2-6. After 14 advances, when the domino reaches the 6 at the end of the train, it is no longer possible to collect 500 points from the roto-target for hitting any panel other than the star, but instead, both top saucers and both out lanes will be lit for a Special.

Bonus?

There is no end of ball bonus. Progress on the domino train does carry over from ball to ball. Tilt ends game.

Differences seen on PlayMates and Grande Domino, the add-a-ball versions

The top lanes and lower standup targets each form a set of numbers 1-6. If no number is lit, roll through a numbered lane or hit a numbered target to select that number, which lights it. Roto-target panels always score 50 points; hitting a star panel or hitting the panel that matches the currently selected number scores an extra ball and de-selects the currently selected number. Lit top lanes or side standup targets score 200 points isntead of 100 on Domino, but there can only be up to one lit at a time. The only way to change the selected number is to collect an extra ball from the roto-target or shoot an upper saucer to reset the selection, allowing a new number to be chosen from the top lanes or side targets. Top saucers only score 200 points instead of Domino's 500, but they're still the safest and most score-efficient things to shoot for.

On Grande Domino, extra balls are treated as an extended play feature by launching the ball upwards from the outhole between the flippers and back into play, whereas on PlayMates, extra balls are sent to the shooter lane to be plunged.
