Black Velvet, Camel Lights, Foxy Lady, Real, and Chuck-a-Luck (Game Plan, 1978)

These are 5 different versions of the same game, rebranded to promote different cigarettes (or, in Chuck-a-Luck's case, to hastily cover up the cigarette branding). These games have identical playfields, rules, and scoring.

Quick strategy synopsis

Shoot the ball up to the top of the playfield via the spinner lanes all day. Making any saucer spins the wheel set into the table, and is the only way to light the left out lane extra ball or activate double bonus. Rollover buttons labelled "Advance" increase the bonus. Rollover buttons labelled "Change" alternate which spinner is lit for 1,000 per spin. Making all 3 saucers in one ball lights the right out lane for Special, but don't shoot the B and C saucers directly, as this adds too much dangerous horizontal momentum to the ball. Be mindful of the oddly shaped slingshots.

Shots and table features

Top of the table

The top of the table includes 3 top lanes, but the ball can also come down the table on either side of the set of 3 lanes. The left and right top lanes score 1,000 points and a bonus advance. The rollover buttons to the sides of the top lanes score 1,000 points. The rollover button above the center top lane scores 1,000 points, 1 bonus advance, and alternates which spinner is lit. The center top lane includes the A saucer, worth 500 points and a wheel spin.

Pop bumpers always score 100 points.

Wheel spins

The A saucer is located in the center top lane, and the B and C saucers are on the sides of the table just above the in lanes. Making a saucer scores 500 points, lights that saucer, and spins the wheel in the center of the playfield. The wheel contains 12 equal sized wedges, with awards distributed as follows:

  • 3 wedges light the Extra in the left out lane (one extra ball)
  • 2 wedges each score 500, 1,000, and 5,000 points
  • 2 wedges award Double Bonus for the current ball
  • 1 wedge awards Light Special in the right out lane

Special can also be lit in the right out lane by lighting all 3 saucers in the same ball.

Spinners

Spinners score 100 points per spin, or 1,000 per spin when lit. Exactly one spinner is always lit. Making any of the three Advance & Change rollover buttons- near the playfield wheel or above the center top lane- alternates which spinner is lit.

Bottom of the table

These games have a mostly usual in/out lane setup, with one out lane, one in lane, one slingshot, and one flipper on each side. The slingshots themselves are quite far from the flippers, but include an extrusion containing only a bouncy rubber and no kicking slingshot which extends to the flipper hinges. In lanes score 1,000 points and a bonus advance. Out lanes advance the bonus, but score no points directly. Spinning Lites Extra on the wheel lights the left out lane for extra ball. Spinning Lite Special on the wheel or making all three saucers in the same ball lights the right out lane for a Special.

Bonus and bonus multiplier

Bonus is advanced by pressing the rollover button above the top lane, pressing either rollover button near the wheel, or rolling through the left or right top lanes. The first bonus advance is not given for free; it is possible to drain with 0 bonus. Double bonus is only given as a wheel award. Max bonus is 2x 19,000 = 38,000 points. Bonus cannot be collected mid-ball and there is no way to carry over base bonus or multiplier from ball to ball.
