Tom Tom (Williams, 1963)

Not to be confused with The Who's Tommy Pinball Wizard (Data East, 1994) or Theatre of Magic (Bally Williams, 1995, and commonly abbreviated as ToM).

Game rules

The swinging targets can score 10, 50, or 100 points. Their value is preserved across players, balls, and games. There are two ways to change the value of the swinging targets.

  • Making 5 Advances will increase the value of both swinging targets. Advances are earned by hitting the red passive bumper at the top of the game or pressing the lit rollover button near the bottom. Lit rollover buttons score 10 points, or 100 for the top rollover button. When the top rollover button is lit, the red passive bumper is lit for 100 points instead of 10 as well, and hitting either one will increase the value of both swinging targets in the sequence 10-50-100. Swinging targets that already have a value of 100 will reset back to 10.
  • The kickers behind the left and right flipper hinges score 5 points and will increase the value of the right and left swinging targets respectively, but only if the corresponding swinging target is not already at 100 points.

Green bumpers score 1 point, or 10 when lit. They are lit by the On Bumpers button in the pop bumper nest, and turned off by the rollover buttons near the slingshots. The yellow bumpers labelled Jet always score 1 point.

There are no in or out lanes. On each side of the bottom of the playfield is a flipper, a kicker, and a slingshot. Drains can only occur between the flippers, but the flipper gap is very large by modern standards and there is no center post. There is no end of ball bonus or extra ball feature. Tilt ends game, but only for the player who tilted.
