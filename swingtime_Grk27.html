Swing Time (Williams, 1963)

Game rules

Letters in Time can only be earned from the 5 top lanes, making a precise plunging and nudging very important early in a ball. All 5 top lanes score 100 points. The 1st, 2nd, 4th, and 5th top lanes from left award the letters T-I-M-E respectively. The center top lane is lit for extra ball on the very first ball of the game, but has no other additional scoring features associated with it.
Collecting T or E lights the left or right out lane respectively for 200 points instead of 100. Collecting I or M lights the green (right) or red (left) pop bumper respectively for 10 points instead of 1. Earning all 4 letters in Time lights the yellow (center) pop bumper for 100 points instead of 10 and scores 1 extra ball.

The passive bumpers in the lower left and lower right as well as the wall switches on either side of the center saucer move a white light clockwise around the diamond in the center of the playfield. The four letters in T-I-M-E are positioned at the left, top, right, and bottom of this diamond respectively. When the moving white light is next to a letter in Time that has been earned, landing in the center saucer scores 1 extra ball and unlights the Time letter next to the white light. At any other time, the center saucer scores 50 points.
An ideal strategy would be to collect all of the letters in Time, advance the white light to any letter, then alternate between 1) collecting extra balls by shooting the center saucer and 2) collecting extra balls by completing Time at the top lanes, i.e., recollecting the letter that was lost when the center saucer extra ball was scored, all while avoiding Advances whenever possible.

There are no in lanes. Flippers back up directly to the slingshots, which score 1 point. Out lanes score 100 points, or 200 if T (left) or E (right) is currently collected. There is no kickback, center post, free ball gate, or end of ball bonus. Multiple extra balls can be earned up to a limit of 10 total balls remaining at any given time. Tilting the game ends the current ball in play and also subtracts one further ball from your total.
