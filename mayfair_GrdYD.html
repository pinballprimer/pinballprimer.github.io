Mayfair / Hyde Park (Gottlieb, 1966)

Mayfair is the replay version. Hyde Park is the add-a-ball version. This guide focuses on Mayfair, with differences observed in Hyde Park listed at the end of the guide.

Mayfair game rules

Rollover lanes score 5 points and advance the selected bonus (red or yellow) when they are not lit, or 50 points and 5 advances of the selected bonus when lit. 1-point switch hits alternate which lanes are lit as well as which bonus is selected. The center standup target can be in either the left or right position; in the left position, hitting the target scores and resets the red bonus, and in the right position, hitting the target scores and resets the yellow bonus. In either position, hitting the target causes it to move to the opposite position. 1 advance of a bonus is equivalent to 10 points, and each bonus individually maxes out at 100 points. If both bonuses are maxed out at 100 points, Double Bonus is lit, meaning the next hit to the center target scores 200 points instead of 100. A shot to the center structure that misses the moving target scores 5 points and advances both bonuses once.

The center bumper always scores 10 points. Outer bumpers score 1 point, or 10 when lit. Bumpers are lit by going through either upper side lane, whether they are lit for 50 points and 5 bonus advances or not. Bumpers are turned off by rolling through the out lanes near the tips of the flippers.

Flippers on Mayfair are very far apart. There is one out lane outside the flippers on each side, and 4 lanes in between them. In the center of the table bottom are two kickers which score 5 points and fire the ball directly upwards toward the moving target. The other 4 lanes are out lanes; they score 50 points and 5 bonus advances when lit, and are lit any time the score motor is not running. (If you quickly drain down an out lane while a bonus is still counting down, you get nothing from the out lane itself.) The near out lanes by the tips of the flippers also turn the bumpers off. It is possible to drain under a raised flipper and not get the awards from any of the out lanes.

The bonuses are never collected at the end of the ball. Bonus levels and whether or not the bumpers are lit are retained from ball to ball, player to player, and game to game. There is no extra ball feature or playfield Special. Tilt ends the ball in play only.

Differences in Hyde Park, the add-a-ball version

Rules and features in this section were verified by Patrick Fifield (he/him) by way of Steel Valley Pinball.

The top lanes are labelled with the letters A-B-C-D-E, and always score 50 points. Make a lit lane to unlight it. The three pop bumpers from left to right are lit if and only if A, C, and E are collected, respectively. Collecting A also lights both left out lanes, and collecting E also lights both right out lanes. Collecting all 5 letters scores 1 extra ball and relights the letters, which in turn unlights the pop bumpers and out lanes. A-B-C-D-E letters reset at the start of each game, but never reset mid-game unless completed, so progress on earned A-B-C-D-E letters can be stolen by your opponent in a 2-player game.

The upper side lanes score 5 points, or 50 when lit, and alternate being lit based on 1-point switches. Likewise, the slingshots score 1 point or 10 when lit and alternate the same way.

The only way to earn Advances in the center column is to hit the center standup target. The wall switch behind the target scores 10 points. Each red or yellow advance scores 100 points. If the target is on the left, hitting it will advance red and move the target to the right, and vice verse for the yellow column if the target is hit in the right position. 1-point switch hits alternate whether Red Double Advance or Yellow Double Advance is lit: hitting the target on the same side as the lit Double Advance causes the target to score 200 points and advance the respective column twice. Advancing a colour past the top of its column scores 1 extra ball. The exact position of the red and yellow columns carries over from ball to ball, player to player, and game to game.

This is a 2-player add-a-ball game. Earned extra balls are indicated by the five coloured dots just below the Hyde Park logo in the lower-left of the backglass. The game will only advance to the next player and/or decrement the balls in play counter if the ball drains with no extra ball dots lit. Up to 5 extra balls can be held in reserve at any one time. Like on Mayfair, tilt ends the ball in play only.
